A different Slot Machine. Educational Project in Digital Humanities

Developing online games with Processing

In 2018 ho proseguito In 2018 I continued my experience as Tutor in the Digital Humanities class held by Prof. Warglien and once again I was in charge of the final project of the students of the course.

In the realization of the final project iour main goal has always been to make the students use all the knowledge and skills developed during the course. In the second year the project developed in an even more creative way and gained international resonance.

As in the previous year's experience, students were involved in all phases of the project: as a result we created a creative online game developed in Processing p5.js. The process involved them from conception to creation and proved to be extremely entertaining, stimulating and creative.

To make the project more concrete for the students, we developed a collaboration withScience Gallery Venicethe museum organization involved the students of the International College of Ca' Foscari in the organization of a workshop for the YOUTH SYMPOSIUM ofScience Gallery International. After a phase of presentation of the reality and the initiative, the final request made to the students was to create an online game on the theme of CO-CREATION.

5/SENSE GENERATOR - GIOCO ONLINE CREATIVO

Inspired by Gianni Rodari's, "Grammar of Fantasy", we decided to focus on the power of our imagination. Starting from the assumption that imagination is a powerful key factor when imagining one's future, we asked ourselves how it works. How are we inspired in our daily lives and how can we combine our (different) visions of things? We thought Come siamo ispirati nella nostra vita quotidiana e come possiamo combinare le nostre (diverse) visioni delle cose? Abbiamo pensato "let's start at the beginning", when things around us trigger our imagination.

Gianni Rodari said that "A stone thrown into a pond produces concentric waves that propagate on its surface, involving in their movement, at different distances, with different effects, the water lily and the rod, the paper boat and the fisherman's float". [...] Not unlike a word, thrown into the mind at random, it produces surface and depth waves, causing an infinite series of chain reactions, involving sounds and images, analogies and memories, meanings and dreams in a movement that influences experience and memory, imagination and the unconscious".

We have chosen to explore not only the words, but also the smells, sounds and all the stimuli that can be encountered in everyday life. These stimuli are the main fuel of our imagination, but each of us has a different perception of the stimuli we face.

To share this reflection, we created a slot machine that generates random stimuli (sounds, images, smells, words, objects to touch...) on the basis of which, the organizers of the Science Gallery Venice workshop would ask the participants to create a story based on these stimuli.

PP5.js is a (simplified) programming language based on javascript. P5.js is an open source programming language built for the electronic arts, new media art and visual design communities with the aim of teaching the basics of computer programming in a visual context and serving as a basis for electronic sketchbooks. It generates html pages that can be uploaded to a server and used as web pages. It can also be "automatically translated" into java, Phyton and javascript code.
CDeveloped skills:: familiarizing processing environment, figure creation, conditional use (if... then), animations, Loops and Arrays, Functions, image manipulation, library integration, tracing and moving an object through color, text encoding, visualization and analysis, XML, introduction to DOM: to create interactive web pages.

The project was presented on the occasion of the second edition of the Youth Symposium of Science Gallery International an initiative that reunites young people from the entire international Science Gallery Network. The theme of the second edition was CO-CREATION.
