Interactive Table. Educational Project in Digital Humanities

Working as Tutor of Digital Humanities

NIn 2017 I worked as Tutor in the Digital Humanities class held by Prof. Warglien and w as mainly responsible for the final project of the students of the course.

In the final project our main goal was to make the students use all the knowledge and skills developed during the course, so I proposed to develop an interactive table based on my applied research Coordinated Galaxies of Culture.

Presented the research, which collected and investigated collaborative projects between 13 Venetian research and cultural institutions, students were involved in all phases of the design of an interactive program developed in Processing. The process saw them as protagonists from conception to realization and turned out to be an extremely entertaining, stimulating and creative project.

Following their suggestions, we decided to build an interactive table representing the city of Venice and the related settlements of the cultural and research institutions that are part of the DVRI to explore the relationships in the District Network.

Adopting an approach aimed at dissemination and gamification, the "cultural city"/"cultural galaxy" was represented as a map to be reassembled: the users had the role of relocating the 13 cultural organizations in the appropriate locations, which were reproduced as 3D printed tokens by Nicol√≤ Merendino.

Moving the token on the physical table also moved the connections on the screen. Each time a token was placed in the correct position, a multimedia content narrated the cultural projects or research projects developed by the DVRI bodies.

Processing is an open source programming language and integrated development environment (IDE) built for the electronic arts, new media art and the visual design community.
Developed skills: familiar processing environment, figure creation, conditional use (if... then), animations, Loops and Arrays, Functions, image manipulation, library integration, tracing and moving an object through color, text encoding, visualization and analysis, XML, introduction to OSC protocol.

The project was presented during the DVRI Social Event at the CNR ISMAR headquarter in Venice.
